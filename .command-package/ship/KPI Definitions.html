KPI Definitions

Twelve headline metrics Command tracks for every agent. Precise definitions, formulas, where the data comes from, how often it refreshes, and what a good number looks like — so the KPI Reporting screens are never ambiguous.

Contents

1 · Acquisition

How new potential business enters the system.

K01
New Leads (per week)
Definition
Any contact that enters the system with a buying/selling intent signal — OH sign-in, form fill, direct referral, expired scrape, manual add with "lead" tag. Excludes past clients and existing SOI.
Formula
COUNT(Contact WHERE created_at in [week start, week end] AND source != 'past_client' AND intent_score >= 2)
Source
Contact table · source attribution from UTM / OH sign-in / manual tag
Refresh
Real-time on insert · daily rollup to dashboard
Target
Benchmark: Strong agent: 8–12/wk · Top agent: 20+/wk · Team lead: 35+/wk
Gotchas
A contact who fills two forms counts once. Double-counting is prevented by match_on_phone_or_email.
K02
Cost Per Lead (CPL)
Definition
Total money spent on marketing / prospecting divided by leads created in that window. Applied by channel when available.
Formula
SUM(spend.amount WHERE date in window) / COUNT(Lead WHERE created_at in window)
Source
Vistaprint orders · Blotato boosted posts · Google / Meta ads · EDDM postage · farm mailer · print jobs
Refresh
Monthly (invoicing-dependent) · nightly for owned spend
Target
Benchmark: $25–$75 is healthy for residential. Under $20 is either a unicorn channel or SOI mispricing.
Gotchas
Branding spend (signs, logos) does not count — only direct-lead spend.
K03
Lead Source Mix
Definition
Distribution of leads by source across a trailing window. Used to spot over-concentration and find declining channels early.
Formula
GROUP BY Lead.source · COUNT(*) / SUM(COUNT(*)) · trailing 90 days
Source
Lead.source enum: open_house | referral | past_client | online_form | cold_prospect | expired | fsbo | iBuyer | other
Refresh
Daily · rolling 90-day window
Target
Rule of thumb: No single source should exceed 40%. Referrals + past clients combined should be >30% for a mature practice.

2 · Engagement

Quality of contact — are you showing up, fast enough, often enough?

K04
Median Response Time
Definition
Median elapsed time from lead-received event to first outbound attempt (call, SMS, or email) logged in Command or via Lofty mirror.
Formula
MEDIAN(Outreach.first_attempt_at - Lead.created_at) WHERE Lead.created_at in window
Source
Lofty mirror (lofty_sync_events) + Command-native outreach log + auto-logged drip replies
Refresh
Real-time · surfaces in the daily digest
Target
Gold standard: <5 min for online-form leads · <30 min for open-house sign-ins · <4 hr for scraped expireds.
Gotchas
AI-drafted auto-responses count only if sent, not queued. "Lead received" timestamp comes from the earliest of (form-submit, first SMS, OH-signin-at).
K05
Contact Velocity
Definition
Average touches per contact per month across all channels (call, text, email, DM, postcard, gift, in-person).
Formula
SUM(touches in month) / COUNT(contacts with activity) — by segment (SOI / leads / past clients)
Source
Outreach + Lofty mirror + call log + email sends + drip auto-logs + SMS
Refresh
Daily
Target
SOI: 1.5+ / mo · Active buyers: 3+ / mo · Past clients: 0.8+ / mo · Cold leads: 1+ / mo until warm-or-drop.
K06
Touch Streak
Definition
Longest consecutive window (weeks) in which a contact received at least one meaningful touch. "Meaningful" excludes bulk newsletter opens.
Formula
MAX consecutive weeks WHERE contact had outreach event with channel in (call, text, dm, in_person, gift)
Source
Outreach with channel tags
Refresh
Daily
Target
For top-30 SOI: aim for a 12-week streak. Command flags "slipped streaks" on the Prospect screen.

3 · Pipeline

The work in motion — value, conversion, and drag.

K07
Active Pipeline ($)
Definition
Sum of expected GCI across all in-flight deals, weighted by stage probability.
Formula
SUM(Deal.expected_gci × Deal.stage_probability) WHERE Deal.stage NOT IN (closed, lost)
Source
Deal table · stage_probability from the config table below
Stage probabilities
StageProbNotes
Prospect10%lead scored > 6
Appointment25%BBA / listing appt scheduled
Active50%BBA signed / listing active
Offer70%offer submitted or received
Under contract92%post-inspection · post-loan-contg if relevant
Closing week98%walkthrough scheduled
Target
Pipeline $ should be ~3× your annual GCI goal divided by 12 at any given point (ratio of 3:1 monthly run).
K08
Stage Conversion Rate
Definition
Percentage of deals that move from one stage to the next within a trailing 90-day window. Measured per stage transition.
Formula
COUNT(Deal WHERE moved from stage A → B) / COUNT(Deal WHERE ever in stage A in window)
Source
Deal.stage_history (append-only log)
Refresh
Nightly
Target
Prospect → Appt: 8–15% · Appt → Active: 50–70% · Active → UC: 30–50% · UC → Close: 90%+
K09
Median Stage Time
Definition
Median days a deal spends in each stage. Identifies the drag points.
Formula
MEDIAN(exited_at - entered_at) GROUP BY stage WHERE exited_at IS NOT NULL
Source
Deal.stage_history
Refresh
Nightly
Target
Appt: 1–3 d · Active (listing): 14–45 d · Active (buyer): 14–90 d · UC: 21–45 d

4 · Revenue

What actually closed — the money truth.

K10
GCI (Gross Commission Income)
Definition
Agent's total commission on closed deals, before cap / splits / fees. Tracked YTD, QTD, MTD.
Formula
SUM(Deal.sale_price × Deal.commission_pct) WHERE Deal.closed_at in window
Source
Deal.closed_at + Deal.sale_price (from final CD) + commission_pct from agreement
Refresh
At close (triggered by CD upload)
Target
Dana's 2025 plan: $1.1M GCI · tracked weekly against the Annual Goals tab.
Gotchas
Referral-outbound deals show net after referral fee. Team leads see both gross and team-net.
K11
Close Rate (List side)
Definition
Listings that close vs listings taken — measures both your pricing discipline and buyer-pool strength.
Formula
COUNT(Listing WHERE closed = true) / COUNT(Listing WHERE taken_at in window)
Source
Listing.status · trailing 12 months to smooth seasonality
Refresh
Nightly
Target
Strong: 75%+ · Great: 90%+ · If under 60%, Command flags pricing or marketing as the likely cause.
K12
Sale-to-List Ratio
Definition
Final sale price divided by original list price (not any reduced list). Measures pricing accuracy and negotiation.
Formula
Deal.sale_price / Listing.original_list_price · averaged over listings closed in window
Source
Listing.original_list_price (captured at list) + Deal.sale_price at close
Refresh
At close
Target
98–102% is healthy · <95% indicates you're listing too high · >105% usually means a hot market OR bidding war

5 · Content

The marketing flywheel — output, reach, and conversion.

K13
Publish Cadence
Formula
COUNT(ContentPost WHERE published_at in week) GROUP BY platform
Target
4+ posts/week on primary platform · daily stories · 1 blog/mo
K14
Reach-per-Follower
Formula
SUM(post.reach) / follower_count_at_post_time
Target
>20% means the algorithm likes you · <10% means post consistency or hooks need work.
K15
Post-to-Lead Conversion
Formula
COUNT(Lead WHERE source = 'social' OR utm_campaign = ContentPost.id) / COUNT(ContentPost)
Target
Avg: 1.5% — above 3% is exceptional · attribution from bio links + UTM on all shortlinks.
K16
Engagement Rate
Formula
(likes + comments + shares + saves) / reach · per post
Target
IG: 3%+ · FB: 1.5%+ · TikTok: 5%+ · LinkedIn: 2%+

6 · Operations

What Command itself saves and costs you.

K17
Auto-time Saved (per week)
Definition
Estimated hours Command automated away, calibrated per action type.
Formula
SUM(action.estimated_manual_minutes) / 60 WHERE action.automated_by = 'command' AND completed_at in week
Source
Estimated minutes table (manual baselines): draft email = 8m · draft social post = 12m · schedule print order = 9m · inspection response = 45m · OH setup = 120m · comp pull = 22m · draft CMA = 40m.
Target
Dana's 2025 plan: 12 hr/week saved. Shown in the weekly digest and the Morning dashboard.
K18
AI Cost Per Deal
Definition
Total token spend (LLM + image + audio) per deal closed, for ROI accountability.
Formula
SUM(ai_cost WHERE deal_id = X) / 1 · then averaged across closed deals in window
Source
ai_usage table keyed on deal_id when attributable, prorated otherwise.
Refresh
Nightly
Target
Healthy: < $40 / deal · Generous: < $100 / deal · (Dana's deal average: $28).
K19
Agreement Cycle Time
Definition
Days from draft to fully-signed for BBAs and listing agreements.
Formula
MEDIAN(Agreement.signed_at - Agreement.drafted_at)
Source
Agreement table · BoldSign timestamps
Target
BBA: <24 hr · Listing: <72 hr

Measurement principles

Five rules we apply when wiring up any new KPI:

Principle 1
Every metric must have a human unit. Percentages for rates, dollars for money, minutes for time, counts for counts. No dimensionless "scores" on the main dashboards — those belong behind a "Methodology" click.
Principle 2
Trailing windows over point-in-time. Single-week numbers are too noisy for a solo agent. Default window is trailing 90 days with a weekly snapshot; annual goals are the exception.
Principle 3
Show the benchmark next to the number. A raw KPI without context is almost meaningless; Command shows benchmark ranges on every card.
Principle 4
Attribution is explicit, never clever. If we can't confidently say a lead came from a source (UTM, form ID, OH sign-in), it's tagged other — we don't guess.
Principle 5
All historical data is immutable. Stage history, touches, and closed deals are append-only. Corrections are entered as new events with a correction=true flag.
