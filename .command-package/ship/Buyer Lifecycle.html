Buyer Lifecycle

Ten stages — from first whisper of buying intent to post-close referral. What defines each stage, what the buyer needs from Dana, what Command automates, and where the "magic buyer letter" fits when inventory is thin.

The ten stages
01. Suspect — curiosity, not commitment
02. Discovery — first real conversation
03. Qualification — lender + BBA
04. Active hunt — showings, saved searches
05. Nothing-fits — magic buyer letter territory
06. Offer prep — comps, terms, strategy
07. Offer submitted — waiting, counter, accept
08. Under contract — due diligence to close
09. Closing week — walkthrough to keys
10. Post-close — home-owning nurture
Suspect → Discovery → Qualified → Active ⇄ Nothing-fits → Offer → UC → Close → Post-close
01
STAGE ONE

Suspect

"Someday I might buy a house."
Who
Anyone who signals interest but hasn't had a real conversation — IG follower asking a DM, OH walk-in, downloaded a buyer's guide, clicked a listing in an email.
Signals
DM reply · IG follow after a buyer-pillar post · OH sign-in with "just looking" · bio-link form fill
Needs
Permission to browse without commitment. Low-pressure value: neighborhood guides, "what's the market like right now," first-time buyer education.
Duration
Indefinite — anywhere from days to years.
Command does
Auto-enrolls into the "nurture" drip (monthly helpful content, not sales). Runs a weekly reach-out tracker. Suggests "move to Discovery" when any signal fires: saved a specific property, replied to a drip, attended 2nd OH.

02
STAGE TWO

Discovery

"I'm thinking seriously. Tell me how this works."
Who
Suspects who've had a real conversation — phone, in-person, or substantive text exchange about their timeline, budget, and neighborhood.
Signals
Replied to a nurture email with a question · called in response to a post · "Hey Dana do you have time this week" DM · 2nd OH sign-in
Needs
Clarity on the process. Honest read on their budget vs their wishlist. Trust that Dana is on their side before they disclose financials.
Duration
1–3 weeks typically. Longer if they're pre-first-conversation.
Graduates to Qualification when: they agree to speak with a lender OR explicitly say "we're ready to start looking seriously."
Exits to Nurture when: timeline > 6 months or no activity for 3 weeks.
Command does
Drafts a buyer consultation invite + 15-min prep doc ("here are the 4 questions I'll ask"). Attaches a trusted-lender list with 3 options. Sets a 48-hr follow-up reminder if no response.
KPI · Median response time KPI · Stage conversion
03
STAGE THREE

Qualification

"Pre-approved. BBA signed. Let's hunt."
Who
A buyer with a pre-approval letter in hand, a signed BBA, and a well-defined target: price band, neighborhood, must-haves vs nice-to-haves.
Gate
Three artifacts must exist in Command: PreApproval.pdf, BBA.signed, and BuyerProfile with criteria.
Needs
A real-time picture of inventory. Confidence that Dana knows what's coming to market before the MLS. A private plan for offer strategy before the first showing.
Duration
Usually 2–7 days to complete the qualification handshake.
Command does
Saved search auto-created from BuyerProfile. BBA e-sign via BoldSign. Sends the Mitchells-style "here's what I think will work for you this week" first-match email. Tracks pre-approval expiration (auto-nudges at 60 days).

04
STAGE FOUR

Active hunt

"Seeing houses. Some fit, some don't."
Who
A qualified buyer actively touring. Has seen 1–8 homes. Offer possible at any moment.
Signals
Requested showings · saved listings · rejected 2+ homes for specific reasons · upcoming showing route
Needs
Fast, curated feed of new listings. Feedback loop after every showing. Per-home pros/cons Dana captures on the road.
Duration
2 weeks (hot market) to 6+ months (cold market or picky buyer).
Command does
New-listing push to buyer's phone when their saved search fires. Route optimization for multi-home tours. In-showing notes app with voice-to-text. Post-tour summary email auto-drafted with pros/cons per property. Sends the buyer a shareable "your search so far" report weekly.

05
STAGE FIVE · THE BRANCH POINT

Nothing-fits

"They want this block. Nothing on the block is for sale."
Who
A qualified, motivated buyer whose criteria — especially neighborhood — cannot be satisfied by current MLS inventory. This is where Dana manufactures supply.
Trigger
Buyer has seen 5+ homes or been active 4+ weeks or criteria has <3 matches in MLS or buyer explicitly says "nothing is coming up."
Needs
A way to create inventory. This is where the Magic Buyer Letter campaign fits.
THE MAGIC BUYER LETTER

When the buyer loves a neighborhood with nothing for sale.

Dana defines a target neighborhood (polygon on a map, or "all 3/2+ in Olive Park under $800k"). Command generates a personalized letter — "A family I'm working with wants to live on your street. They love it because…" — prints it, and Dana (or her assistant) walks every surrounding home. This is the highest-conversion off-market-supply mechanism in real estate.

The flow

StepWhoWhat
1. TriggerDana or CommandFlag buyer as "nothing-fits" · define target polygon on the Properties map
2. Parcel pullCommandPulls every non-listed parcel in the polygon · filters by buyer's criteria (3/2+, lot size, year built) · auto-skips current listings
3. Letter draftCommandPersonalized letter: "The Parks are looking for a 3/2 in Olive Park. They have two kids and love your block because…"
4. Buyer approvesBuyerOne-click review of the letter (to preserve authenticity). Buyer can edit before print.
5. Print sourceDana picksTitle company print program · local printer · DIY — see Print & Delivery doc
6. DeliveryDana walks itCommand generates a walking route · target ~45 homes in 60 minutes · door-hanger + personal touch · SMS Dana reminder the night before
7. Response trackingCommandQR on letter routes responses to Dana's private line · tracks every reply · auto-logs who called vs who didn't
8. Match to buyerDana + buyerInterested seller? Dana sets up a private tour for the buyer — zero-days-on-market, zero-competition offer.
Typical conversion
50–80 letters generally produces 1–3 seller replies, of which 1 typically results in a private showing, and 1 in 3 of those becomes a deal. Total cycle: 2–6 weeks. Cost: ~$75 (letters + print) vs. ~$18,000 in GCI on a typical close.
Command also does (beyond the letter)
Suggests adjacent neighborhoods that match criteria. Alerts Dana on pre-MLS leads (FSBO posts, Nextdoor "thinking of selling" posts, expired listings matching criteria). Runs a weekly "nothing-fits" review so no buyer gets lost here.

06
STAGE SIX

Offer prep

"This is the one. Let's structure the offer."
Who
Active buyer with a target property they want to pursue — either from their MLS-active search OR from a successful magic-letter match.
Gate
Buyer has said "write an offer" (or equivalent).
Needs
Fast comp analysis. Offer-strategy coaching. Reassurance about non-price terms (earnest money, contingencies, timeline).
Duration
2–48 hours. In hot markets: minutes.
Command does
Pulls 6 tight comps (<0.5mi, <90d, similar sf/bed/bath). Drafts offer package via Transactly / DocuSign. Suggests terms based on: listing DOM, comps sale-to-list, competing offers if known. Runs a "would you lose this for $5k?" scenario analysis.
KPI · Stage time KPI · Close rate (buyer side)
07
STAGE SEVEN

Offer submitted

"We sent it. We wait."
Who
Offer has been submitted; awaiting response from listing side.
Signals
Offer acknowledged · counteroffer received · multi-offer situation · accepted
Needs
Real-time status, help reading counteroffers, emotional support during bidding wars.
Duration
Hours to days.
Command does
Pings Dana the moment a counter hits her email (via parsing). Drafts counter-response with strategy brief: "They moved $10k. Competing offers likely gone. I'd counter at X because…" Real-time buyer-text draft to soothe nerves.
08
STAGE EIGHT

Under contract

"Due diligence to loan clear."
Who
Mutually signed purchase agreement. In the ~30-day window to close.
Milestones
EMD · Inspection · Inspection-response deadline · Appraisal · Loan contingency · Walkthrough · Close
Needs
Calendar certainty. A single source of truth for every deadline. Vendor coordination. Inspection-response negotiation.
Duration
21–45 days typically.
Command does
Reads the contract PDF, extracts all 14 deadlines, loads them onto the Deal cockpit. Coordinates with lender (loan docs), title (commitment order), home-warranty, insurance, HOA. Drafts the inspection-response letter based on findings. Sends the Mitchells the "what to expect this week" email every Monday.
KPI · Stage time (UC) KPI · Auto-time saved
09
STAGE NINE

Closing week

"Five days. Utility switch, final walkthrough, keys."
Who
Final stretch: 5–7 days before close.
Needs
Utility transfers, moving coordination, walkthrough orchestration, closing day logistics, first-night bag.
Duration
~1 week.
Command does
Auto-checks each utility switch (power, gas, water, internet). Reminds buyers about homeowner's insurance effective-date. Walkthrough checklist delivered the morning of. Drafts thank-you + closing-gift logistics (local honey + bread). Schedules the "after-keys" text: "How's the first night?"
10
STAGE TEN

Post-close

"Two years of staying top-of-mind."
Who
Past buyers — your long-term moat. The most valuable contacts in your database.
Needs
A feeling of being remembered. Useful homeownership help. An invitation to refer, without the ask being transactional.
Duration
Forever, at decreasing cadence.
Command does
  • Day 30: "How's the house?" text (personal, from Dana)
  • Day 60: Handwritten-style note + local-business discount
  • 6 months: "Your home's current value" email (auto-pulled from comps)
  • 1 year: Home-owning anniversary card · local-biz gift
  • 2 years: Personal check-in call · referral ask in soft form
  • Ongoing: "Your neighborhood this month" — listings, sales, notable events

Principles for buyer work

Principle 1
A stuck buyer is a lost buyer. Command flags any buyer stalled in a stage beyond its typical duration. "Nothing-fits" past 6 weeks is an active intervention moment — probably a magic-letter campaign.
Principle 2
The magic letter is a supply tool, not a marketing tool. It's used for a specific buyer, never as a general farming mechanism. Personalization is the entire point.
Principle 3
Post-close is the moat. 40%+ of a mature agent's business should come from past clients and their referrals. Command's post-close cadence is the product's most valuable surface — never skippable.
Principle 4
Never auto-transition a stage. Every stage change is a human decision in this product. Command suggests, Dana confirms. This matters because stage changes trigger real outreach.
