Arizona advertising compliance

Rules extracted from ADRE's 2026 Law Book, A.A.C. R4-28-502, and the Dec 13 2025 rule revisions. This is the seed corpus for the Command compliance gate. Every outbound piece of content — Reels, Stories, carousels, emails, yard signs, Bio Link, listing descriptions, AI-generated drafts — runs through these rules.

Brokered by REAL Broker, AZ. Dana is a solo agent under REAL Broker. Every rule below assumes the employing broker's legal name or registered DBA must appear. Confirm with REAL's compliance team: the exact legal string to use (e.g. "REAL Broker AZ, LLC" vs "Real Broker, LLC" vs DBA). That string gets stored in settings.broker_legal_name and the compliance checker validates it appears in every piece.

2 · The core rule

A.A.C. R4-28-502 — clear and prominent broker identification. Every advertisement must identify the employing broker's legal name or registered DBA in a clear and prominent manner. Effective Dec 13 2025, this explicitly covers online, electronic, and AI-assisted advertising, and the identification must be visible without requiring the viewer to scroll, tap-to-expand, or click through.

"Advertising" is defined broadly by ADRE: any publication — including social media posts, Reels, Stories, carousels, internet postings, emails, text messages, yard signs, mailers, Zillow/Realtor.com profiles — that offers an interest in property or brokerage services in Arizona. Press releases for general PR are generally exempt; promotional content is not.

3 · Rules in machine-readable form

Each rule below is a single embeddable chunk. The checker retrieves by similarity, runs Sonnet with retrieved rules, emits verdict + cited rule id + plain-English reason.

AZ-ADV-001 · R4-28-502 · broker identification

Clear & prominent broker name

Rule: All advertising must display the employing broker's legal name or registered DBA in a clear and prominent manner.

Applies to: Instagram posts, Reels, Stories, carousels, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube captions + video overlays, Facebook posts, email footers, yard signs, mailers, listing flyers, Zillow/Realtor.com profiles, Bio Link pages, AI-generated ads.

Check: Does the rendered content contain the exact settings.broker_legal_name string, positioned in a visible area of the first-view frame (not requiring scroll/expand)?

BLOCKif missing. WARNif present but in a collapsed "... more" region.
AZ-ADV-002 · Dec 13 2025 revision · no-scroll requirement

No-scroll, no-expand visibility

Rule: As of Dec 13 2025, broker identification may not be placed in a region that requires scrolling, tapping "more," or clicking through a link to view.

Specifics: Instagram caption text auto-truncates at ~125 characters on feed. Broker name must appear before that truncation point. Reels and Stories: broker name must be in on-screen text overlay or visible in the first frame of caption. Bio-only attribution does not satisfy the rule for individual posts.

BLOCKif the broker name appears after character 125 in an Instagram caption without being in the image/video itself.
AZ-ADV-003 · AI-assisted content

AI-generated ads are regulated

Rule: The Dec 13 2025 revisions explicitly bring AI-generated and AI-assisted advertising under R4-28-502. Anything the agent uses AI to draft, image-generate, caption, or translate must still comply with the broker-ID and truthfulness rules.

Broker supervision angle: Designated brokers may be held responsible for AI-generated content produced under their supervision. Dana's brokerage may require her to flag AI-assisted posts.

WARNon all AI-drafted content with an advisory: "AI-drafted — confirm your broker's AI disclosure policy applies."
AZ-ADV-004 · owner permission for property ads

Owner permission required

Rule: Owner permission is required before placing signs or advertising a specific property. Applies to listing teasers, "coming soon" posts, drone footage, and open-house promos.

BLOCKif content references a specific address and Command has no listing_agreement.advertising_consent = true on file for that property.
AZ-ADV-005 · other licensee's listings

Special disclosures when advertising another agent's listing

Rule: Special disclosures apply when advertising another licensee's listings or transaction history. Posting another agent's listing as if it's yours, or claiming a sale you cooperated on without proper disclosure, is a violation.

BLOCKon phrases like "my new listing" / "just sold" when the Command deal record shows Dana was buyer's side or not the listing agent.
AZ-ADV-006 · misleading & guarantee claims

No false or misleading claims

Rule: Agents may not make false, misleading, or deceptive statements. This includes unverifiable superlatives ("#1 agent in Evanston"), value guarantees ("guaranteed to double your home's value"), and timeline promises ("sold in 7 days or your money back") without substantiation.

BLOCKon regex-detected phrases: /guarantee(d)?|#1|top \d+|no\.? ?1|fastest|best in|only agent/i unless accompanied by a substantiation note.
AZ-ADV-007 · material adverse facts

Material adverse facts disclosure (Dec 13 2025 expansion)

Rule: Disclosure duties now extend beyond clients. Material adverse facts must be disclosed even if they are not technical property defects — and may apply to non-clients, including when the agent is acting as a principal.

Content implication: If a piece of marketing omits a known material adverse fact (flood history, structural issue, disclosed contamination), it can trigger a disclosure violation even if the ad itself is technically accurate.

WARNwhen a listing has open items in disclosures.material_facts and marketing omits them.
AZ-ADV-008 · fair housing

Fair Housing Act compliance

Rule: No preference, limitation, or discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (incl. gender identity and sexual orientation per HUD), familial status, national origin, disability. Applies to photos, copy, neighborhood descriptors, demographic claims.

Common traps: "great for young families" (familial status), "walking distance to [faith] community" (religion), "safe neighborhood" (coded), "exclusive," "traditional," language-only ads that exclude groups.

BLOCKon Fair-Housing classifier confidence ≥ 0.7.
AZ-ADV-009 · RESPA §8 referral fees

No kickbacks or referral fees for settlement services

Rule: RESPA §8 prohibits giving or receiving a thing of value for the referral of settlement service business. Agents cannot offer cash rebates, gift cards, or credits for referrals to lenders, title, inspectors, or other settlement providers.

BLOCKon referral-fee language involving any entity tagged as vendor.category IN ('lender','title','inspector','appraiser','escrow').
AZ-ADV-010 · testimonials & reviews

Testimonial truthfulness

Rule: Testimonials must be truthful, represent actual client experience, and cannot be fabricated or materially edited. If a testimonial implies typical results, those results must be substantiated.

WARNon testimonial posts without a testimonials.source_id linking to a logged contact with a signed release.
AZ-ADV-011 · team & DBA identification

Team names must comply

Rule: If Dana operates under a team name ("The Massey Group," etc.), the team name cannot obscure or replace the employing broker's identification. Per Dec 13 2025 revisions, ADRE may require team registration.

Pattern: "The Massey Group · REAL Broker" is OK. "The Massey Group" alone is not.

BLOCKif team name appears without accompanying broker identification in the same frame.
AZ-ADV-012 · IL residency / multi-state ambiguity

Cross-state advertising clarity

Rule: Licensees may only advertise the purchase, sale, or lease of Arizona property if licensed in Arizona. If advertising directed to out-of-state viewers (likely on Instagram), the advertising must not solicit brokerage services outside the licensed state.

WARNif copy targets a non-AZ geography ("Evanston," "Chicago," etc.) and Dana's license jurisdiction is AZ only — surfaces a jurisdictional question.

4 · Checker algorithm

Pseudo-code for lib/compliance/check.ts:

input: { content, type, related_entity_id, author: 'human' | 'ai-drafted' }

1. classify type: social-post · reel-caption · story · email · listing-description · bio-link · yard-sign · mailer
2. build context:
     - broker_legal_name = settings.broker_legal_name
     - property = if related_entity_id → load property + disclosures
     - deal = load deal + sides
     - testimonial_sources = for any quoted reviews
3. deterministic checks first (cheap, no LLM):
     - broker name present → AZ-ADV-001
     - broker name in first 125 chars of social caption → AZ-ADV-002
     - regex for guarantee/superlative → AZ-ADV-006
     - regex for referral-fee to settlement vendor → AZ-ADV-009
     - team name without broker tag → AZ-ADV-011
4. RAG retrieval: top 6 rule chunks from Pinecone compliance index
5. Sonnet call (via lib/ai/bill.ts):
     prompt includes content + context + retrieved rules
     output = { verdict: pass|warn|block, cited_rules: [], reasons: [] }
6. merge deterministic + LLM verdicts; highest severity wins
7. log to compliance_checks table with content hash + verdict + cited chunks
8. if block → post to #compliance Slack with override link
9. return verdict to caller

5 · Override flow

Dana can override a BLOCK verdict with a written justification. The override is logged permanently to compliance_overrides:

table compliance_overrides (
  id uuid pk,
  check_id uuid references compliance_checks,
  overridden_by uuid references users,
  reason text not null,
  broker_notified boolean default true,
  created_at timestamptz
);

Every override also posts to #compliance Slack channel for audit trail. If REAL Broker requires broker approval for overrides, the flow switches to a request-approval pattern (post + wait for broker ✓ reaction).

7 · Settings surface

Add to /admin/integrations/compliance page:

  • Broker legal name — text input. Default blank. Must be set before any content can publish. Suggest: "REAL Broker" with a note asking Dana to confirm with her DB.
  • Team name — optional. If set, checker enforces AZ-ADV-011.
  • Licensed states — multi-select. Used by AZ-ADV-012.
  • AI disclosure required — toggle. If on, AI-drafted content gets a visible "🤖 AI-assisted" tag automatically.
  • Override policy — radio: self-approve · require-broker. Routes the override flow.
  • Rule corpus status — last-ingested timestamps per namespace, with "re-ingest" buttons.

8 · Non-advertising rule exposure

The Dec 13 2025 revisions carry non-advertising items Command should surface elsewhere (not gate content, but remind Dana):

  • 10 calendar-day ADRE reporting — if Dana changes address, phone, email, employment, Command shows a banner in Settings: "You have 10 calendar days to notify ADRE of this change."
  • Promptly-notify-broker if unable to perform — if Dana marks herself unavailable (vacation mode), Command drafts a broker notification Slack DM.
  • Expanded disclosure duties to non-clients — when Dana is acting as a principal (buying/selling her own property), CMA and offer flows inject an extra disclosure step.
