The Science First

Why frameworks work — and what's happening inside the brain

Copywriting frameworks aren't opinions or preferences. They're built on decades of behavioral science research about how humans process information, experience emotion, and make decisions. Before choosing a framework, understand what you're actually activating.

Every framework below operates on one or more of these core psychological levers. The ones that convert the most reliably in real estate — where stakes are high and emotions run hotter than almost any other purchase — are the ones that hit the most levers at once.

2–3×

People are 2 to 3 times more motivated to avoid pain than to gain pleasure. This is Kahneman and Tversky's Nobel Prize-winning Prospect Theory — the core engine behind PAS.

95%

Neuroscience research shows 95% of purchasing decisions are made emotionally, then rationalized logically after the fact. Logic closes deals — emotion opens them.

3–10×

Marketing expert Perry Marshall found that problem-focused messaging generates 3 to 10 times more response than product or company-focused messaging.

01
PAS · Problem · Agitate · Solution

The one you already use — and should keep using

Your Core Formula

Definition

The simplest and most battle-tested persuasion structure in existence. You name the exact pain your reader is feeling, twist it until they feel its full weight, then position yourself as the only sensible exit from it. Dan Kennedy called it "the most reliable sales formula ever invented."

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Activates loss aversion — the most powerful human motivator. Pain is felt 2–3× more intensely than equivalent gain. By making the problem vivid and urgent before offering relief, you create undeniable motivation to act. The Agitate step is the engine. Most people skip it. Don't.

P
Problem
Name the exact pain point. Be specific enough that the reader feels you read their diary. Vague problems get ignored.
A
Agitate
Twist the knife. Amplify what ignoring this problem costs them emotionally, financially, and practically. This is where 90% of copy fails — people skip this step because it feels uncomfortable.
S
Solution
After the problem is felt viscerally, your solution arrives as obvious relief. Present it with authority and a clear next step.

Real Estate Examples by Segment

Expired Sellers

"Your listing expired. Again. You did everything you were told — cleaned, staged, showed up. And it still sat there. The truth is the problem was never the house. It was the pricing strategy and the marketing. I fix both, with a PSA certification and 15 years of digital marketing behind it."

Relocation Buyers

"You're moving to Arizona and you're making every decision based on Zillow, Reddit, and a weekend you visited five years ago. There are five Gilbert zip codes with wildly different schools, HOAs, and price trajectories. You deserve someone who grew up here and came back. That's me."

Investors

"By the time a deal hits Zillow, it's already gone. The good stuff moves before it's listed. If you're still hunting the MLS for investment properties, you're fishing in an empty pond."

Snowbirds / 55+

"You've been renting seasonally for three years while prices climbed. What you paid in rent last season could've been equity. Let's fix that before next winter arrives."

02
PASTOR · Problem · Amplify · Story · Transformation · Offer · Response

PAS with proof — the long-form upgrade

High-Ticket Closer

Definition

Created by copywriter Ray Edwards, PASTOR is what PAS becomes when you have space and a high-stakes offer. It extends the PAS structure with a story, a before/after transformation, your specific offer, and a clear call to action. This is the framework for listing presentations, landing pages, webinars, and full sales sequences.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Adds social proof through Story, which activates mirror neurons — we feel what others felt. The Transformation step creates aspirational identity shift ("this is who I'll become"). Together, PASTOR addresses every objection category: pain, trust, proof, desire, and commitment. It's built for decisions where someone is spending six figures.

P
Problem
Name the core pain or challenge your reader faces.
A
Amplify
Show the consequences of not solving it. Make the cost of inaction real.
S
Story
Share a client story or testimonial that mirrors the reader's situation. They see themselves in it.
T
Transformation
Paint the vivid after — what life looks like on the other side of the solution.
O
Offer
Lay out exactly what you're offering, what's included, and why it's the right path.
R
Response
Give one clear, frictionless call to action. One. Don't give them options.

Real Estate Application

Listing Presentation / Expired

"Problem: Your home sat on the market without selling. Amplify: Every week it sat, buyers assumed something was wrong with it. Price cut anxiety sets in. Your neighbors watched. Story: I worked with a seller in Mesa whose listing expired after 74 days. We relisted with a PSA-backed price and a targeted digital campaign — under contract in 9 days. Transformation: Imagine actually getting to the closing table, check in hand, moving forward. That's the other side of this. Offer: Here's exactly what I do differently…"

Relocation Buyers (Long Email / Blog)

"Problem: Moving across the country is overwhelming — especially when you don't know the market. Amplify: Make the wrong neighborhood call and your kids are in the wrong school district, you're stuck in HOA hell, and you're 40 minutes from where you actually need to be. Story: My last relocation client from California told me they'd been looking online for four months and felt more confused than when they started. We found their home in three weeks. Transformation: Picture landing in Gilbert with a house that fits your life — not just your budget…"

03
AIDA · Attention · Interest · Desire · Action

The original — best for strangers who don't know you yet

Top of Funnel

Definition

The oldest copywriting framework in existence — first documented in 1898 by advertising pioneer E. St. Elmo Lewis. AIDA takes someone from complete unawareness to ready-to-act. It doesn't lead with pain the way PAS does — instead it follows cognitive progression from curiosity to conviction. Best for cold audiences who haven't identified their problem yet.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Maps directly to how the brain processes new information: first notice, then engage, then want, then act. It respects the reader's cognitive journey rather than forcing urgency. Cold audiences need to be led through this progression — skipping steps causes drop-off. AIDA is the framework for SEO content, social awareness posts, and any situation where you're introducing yourself to someone who didn't ask.

A
Attention
A headline or hook that interrupts the scroll. Surprising, specific, or provocative.
I
Interest
Build curiosity by sharing something they don't know, or framing something familiar in a fresh way.
D
Desire
Show them the better version of their situation. Make them want what you have.
A
Action
One specific, frictionless next step. Don't list options.

Real Estate Examples by Segment

General Buyers (Instagram / Blog)

"Gilbert has 4 zip codes. They are not the same. [Attention] Here's what most buyers don't realize until after closing: which one you buy in determines your school district, your HOA structure, your drive to the 202, and your resale in 5 years. [Interest] The 85296 zip has been outperforming its neighbors on appreciation for 3 straight years. [Desire] Want a breakdown of all 4 before you start looking? [Action] DM me 'GILBERT' and I'll send it over."

Relocation Buyers (SEO Blog)

"California to Arizona: What Nobody Tells You Before You Move [Attention] Most people moving from California focus on price. That's smart. But there are five other things that will determine whether you love or hate where you land — and none of them are on Zillow. [Interest] I've helped dozens of California families land in the East Valley without a single regret. [Desire] Here's the full relocation guide I give every one of them. [Action]"

04
BAB · Before · After · Bridge

Transformation selling — show the life they want

Aspiration & Results

Definition

BAB leads with the current situation (Before), then paints a vivid picture of the desired state (After), and finally shows how to get from one to the other (Bridge). Unlike PAS which drives through pain, BAB drives through aspiration — it's the framework for showing results, sharing testimonials, and selling the transformation rather than the transaction.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Creates mental ownership — the reader literally visualizes themselves living in the After state before they've committed. This pre-commitment effect is a documented psychological phenomenon. BAB is particularly powerful in tight formats (social captions, short video, direct mail cards) because its structure is instantly readable even when scanned, not read.

B
Before
Describe the current, less-than-ideal situation the reader is stuck in. Make it relatable, not dramatic.
A
After
Paint the vivid, specific, emotionally resonant life on the other side. This is where you make them want it.
B
Bridge
Your product, service, or approach is the path that gets them from Before to After. Keep this part simple.

Real Estate Examples by Segment

Snowbirds / 55+ (Social / Direct Mail)

"Before: You're renting a place in Scottsdale every winter, paying someone else's mortgage, and leaving in April wondering if prices will be even higher next year. After: You own your piece of Arizona. Your car stays. Your stuff stays. You fly back whenever you want. After: That's 5 years of rental money back in your equity. Bridge: I specialize in 55+ communities across the East Valley. Let's find yours."

Expired Sellers (Client Results Post)

"Before: Listed for 97 days. No offers. One price reduction. Sellers were ready to give up. After: Under contract in 9 days at 98.7% of asking price. Bridge: The difference was a certified pricing strategy (PSA) and a real digital marketing plan. Same house. Different agent. Different result."

05
4Ps · Picture · Promise · Proof · Push

When they're warm — convince them to commit

Conversion & Proof

Definition

The 4Ps framework is a mid-to-bottom funnel structure for audiences who are already aware they have a problem and are evaluating options. It opens with a vivid picture of the desired outcome, makes a bold promise, backs it up with proof (data, testimonials, credentials), and closes with a direct push to act. This is the framework for conversion pages, testimonial posts, and closing sequences.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Leads with desire (Picture) before hitting them with credibility (Promise + Proof), which sidesteps the skepticism that cold logic-first approaches trigger. The Proof step is crucial for real estate where trust is the primary objection — people need to see that someone like them got results. Push works because it reduces friction and provides decision clarity.

P
Picture
Open with a vivid image of the desirable outcome. Sensory, emotional, specific.
P
Promise
Make a clear, bold commitment to deliver that outcome. No hedging.
P
Proof
Back the promise with data, testimonials, certifications, case studies, or track record.
P
Push
Give them the nudge — urgency, scarcity, or a low-friction CTA that makes acting easy.

Real Estate Examples by Segment

Listing Page / Seller Landing Page

"Picture: Your home under contract — real offer, right price, clean timeline. Promise: I will bring you a certified pricing strategy, professional marketing across every relevant channel, and the straight talk most agents are afraid to give you. Proof: PSA-certified. 15+ years digital marketing. Here's what my last three sellers said. Push: Let's schedule a 20-minute call. No pressure, no pitch — just clarity on what your home is worth and what needs to happen."

Investor Outreach (Email / DM)

"Picture: An off-market deal hitting your inbox before it touches the MLS. Promise: I source on-market AND off-market inventory — the stuff other agents don't have access to. Proof: I use Lead Deck to find deals that never get listed publicly. I've closed [X] investor transactions in the East Valley. Push: Tell me your buy box criteria. I'll start running it."

06
StoryBrand · Character · Problem · Guide · Plan · CTA · Success · Failure

Make the client the hero — you're the guide

Brand Clarity

Definition

Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework inverts the typical agent-centric marketing approach. Instead of the agent as hero, the client is the hero and you are the trusted guide (think Gandalf, not Frodo). Your job is to clarify their journey, acknowledge their problem, and give them a simple plan to reach success — while also naming what failure looks like if they don't act. Ideal for brand messaging, website copy, and positioning work.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Most real estate marketing makes the agent the star. StoryBrand's research shows this is a conversion killer — because the brain's primary survival instinct is to be self-focused. When your messaging positions the client as the hero and you as the guide who helps them win, it triggers trust faster than any credential can. People don't buy from experts. They buy from guides who understand them.

C
Character
Who is your client? Define them as the hero with a goal.
P
Problem
External (what they face), Internal (how it makes them feel), Philosophical (why it's unfair).
G
Guide
You. Show empathy (you understand) and authority (you've solved this before).
P
Plan
A simple 3-step process. Remove complexity and decision friction.
CTA
Call to Action
Direct (book a call) and transitional (download the guide). Both matter.

Real Estate Application — Website Hero / About Page

Website Headline Structure

"You deserve an agent with a real marketing strategy — not someone who just posts on Instagram and prays. I'm Dana Massey. I'm a PSA-certified agent and a marketing professional with 15 years of digital experience. Here's how it works: 1) We talk about your situation honestly. 2) I build a strategy that fits it. 3) We execute and get you to the closing table. Don't spend another listing cycle hoping for different results."

Relocation Buyer Version

"You're moving to Arizona and you're trying to make the biggest financial decision of your life in a market you don't know yet. I grew up here, left for a decade, and came back. I know every neighborhood in the East Valley like a local because I am one. Here's how we'll find your home: 1) I learn your life first. 2) I send you on and off-market matches — not just Zillow. 3) You move with confidence."

07
FAB · Features · Advantages · Benefits

When they're already sold — make the offer concrete

Warm Audience / Objection Handling

Definition

FAB answers the question every buyer or seller has in their head: "What does this actually mean for me?" It moves from what a service IS (Feature) to why it matters (Advantage) to what it actually does for the client's life (Benefit). FAB isn't a standalone framework — it's a layer you add to other frameworks to make your offer concrete and credible. Use it inside PASTOR or 4Ps at the offer stage.

Why It Works (The Psychology)

Most agents list features without completing the translation to benefits — which means clients have to do the cognitive work themselves. Most don't. FAB removes that friction by making the "so what" explicit. The benefit is always about their life, not your service. "PSA certified" is a feature. "You won't get a price pulled from thin air" is the benefit. That's the difference.

F
Feature
What it IS — the tangible attribute of the service or offering.
A
Advantage
What it DOES — why this feature matters compared to the alternative.
B
Benefit
What it MEANS FOR THEM — the emotional or practical payoff in their life.

Dana's Services Translated Through FAB

PSA Certification

"Feature: I hold the NAR Pricing Strategy Advisor (PSA) certification. Advantage: My pricing is based on a rigorous data methodology — not comps pulled from Zillow and adjusted by gut feel. Benefit: You don't leave money on the table by pricing too low, and you don't sit on the market by pricing too high. Your price is defensible on day one."

15 Years Digital Marketing

"Feature: 15+ years in digital marketing before real estate. Advantage: I know how to get a listing seen by qualified buyers — not just posted to the MLS and forgotten. Benefit: Your home gets in front of people who are actually looking for exactly what you're selling, which means more showings and better offers, faster."

Off-Market Access (Investors)

"Feature: I use Lead Deck for off-market sourcing. Advantage: You see deals before they hit the MLS — before every other investor in the Phoenix metro is looking at the same property. Benefit: You buy at pre-list pricing, with less competition and more negotiating room."

Concierge / Solo Agent Model

"Feature: I work as a solo concierge agent — you work with me, not a team of assistants. Advantage: No handoffs, no dropped balls, no "your agent is with another client right now." Benefit: You have one person who knows every detail of your transaction from day one to closing and beyond."

Based on audience temperature, attention span, format constraints, and conversion goal. Cold audiences respond to AIDA and PAS. Warm audiences convert better with 4Ps and PASTOR.

Advanced Application

How to layer frameworks — the real pro move

The best copywriters don't pick one framework and live in it. They use PAS or BAB as the hook, then shift frameworks as the content deepens. Here are the combinations that work best for Dana's business.

Instagram Reel
PAS hook → BAB close

How it works: Open with a PAS hook (name the pain in the first 2 seconds), agitate it through the middle of the video, then flip to a BAB close showing the transformation. "Your listing sat. No offers. [Problem] You wonder what you did wrong. [Agitate] My last client in the same situation was under contract in 9 days. [After] Here's the difference. [Bridge + CTA]"

Blog Post
AIDA intro → PASTOR body → 4Ps CTA

How it works: Use AIDA to hook the cold reader and pull them into the post. Shift to PASTOR in the body to build the story, proof, and transformation arc. Close with a 4Ps CTA section — Picture the outcome, Promise what you deliver, offer Proof (testimonial or credential), and Push with a specific low-friction action step.

Listing Presentation
StoryBrand opening → PASTOR body → 4Ps close

How it works: Open with StoryBrand (they're the hero, you're the guide — here's the simple plan). Shift to PASTOR for the bulk of the presentation (amplify what went wrong before, tell a success story, show the transformation). Close with 4Ps — paint the outcome, make the promise, stack the proof, and push to sign the agreement.

Email Sequence (7-part)
Email 1: PAS · 2–4: PASTOR · 5–6: BAB + 4Ps · 7: PAS close

How it works: Open the sequence with a PAS email — high emotion, low commitment. Build trust over emails 2–4 using PASTOR (story, proof, transformation). Emails 5–6 shift to BAB results stories backed by 4Ps proof. Final email returns to PAS urgency — remind them of the problem and give them one clear way out.
